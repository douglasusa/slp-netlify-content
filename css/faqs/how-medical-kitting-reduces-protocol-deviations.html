How does medical kitting reduce protocol deviations?

Medical kitting reduces protocol deviations by ensuring every site uses the same materials, instructions, and labeling, which minimizes variability and improves compliance across clinical trials and research programs.

When kits are pre‑assembled with standardized components, sites no longer need to source materials independently or interpret protocol requirements differently. This reduces the risk of missing items, incorrect supplies, or inconsistent handling procedures.

With SLP CONNECT™, sponsors and CROs gain real‑time visibility into kit usage, inventory, and logistics performance, enabling proactive oversight and reducing the likelihood of deviations across multi‑site and decentralized studies.
