What is kitting and why does it matter?

Kitting is the process of assembling multiple components—products, supplies, instructions, and packaging—into a single, ready‑to‑use unit. In healthcare, retail, clinical research, and consumer goods, kitting is what turns a collection of parts into a usable experience.

When kitting is done well, it reduces errors, speeds up workflows, and creates a consistent experience for clinicians, staff, and customers. When it’s done poorly, it creates confusion, waste, and risk.

Strategic Lab Partners focuses on kitting as a discipline: we design kits so they are intuitive, compliant, and repeatable—and then we back that design with manufacturing, warehousing, and 3PL deployment so the same kit shows up correctly every time.

For organizations scaling programs or entering new markets, kitting is often the difference between a concept that looks good in a slide deck and a program that actually works in the field.
